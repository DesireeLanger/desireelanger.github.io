• Threonyl tRNA synthetase (ThrRS) is an enzyme which attaches threonine to its cognate tRNA:
    • Thr + tRNAthr + ATP → Thr-tRNAThr + AMP + PPi
    Although the class II superfamily phylogeny is not entirely clear, ThrRS is part of subclass IIa alongside HisRS, ProRS, SerRS, and the dimeric GlyRS (Gomez et al. 2020, Valencia-Sánchez et al. 2016, Perona et al. 2012). With the exception of SerRS, the anticodon binding domains of subclass IIa are homologous and reside at the C-terminal (Wolf et al. 1999).

    The catalytic domain of ThrRS is quite typical of a class II aaRS. Like most members of the superfamily, ATP binding is coordinated by the arginine tweezers, located in motifs 2 and 3 (Kaiser et al. 2018). The catalytic domain contains six or seven antiparallel sheets.

    Editing in ThrRS occurs at the post-transfer level. This process has been characterised for the removal of mischarged serines from tRNAThr (Dock-Bregeon et al. 2000). This function is achieved through the two N-termimal domains N1 and N2 (Dock-Bregeon et a. 2004). A non-homologous N-terminal editing domain (which also targets mischarged serine) is present in some archaea instead of the N1 and N2 domains (Hussain et al. 2006). The editing domain of ThrRS is upstream of the catalytic domain, and is homologous with that of AlaRS, which conversely resides downstream of the catalytic domain (Sankaranarayanan et al. 1999).
