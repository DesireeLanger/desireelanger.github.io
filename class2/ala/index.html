• AlaRS catalyses the following reaction:
    • Ala + tRNAAla + ATP → Ala-tRNAAla + AMP + PPi
    The catalytic core of AlaRS is similar to the tetrameric GlyRS found mainly in bacteria. Although the class II superfamily phylogeny is not entirely clear, these two members appear to be closely related, constituting subclass IIc or IId depending on the author (Valencia-Sánchez et al. 2016, Gomez et al. 2020).

    The catalytic domain of AlaRS has seen numerous deletions and stands apart from other class II members. Aside from these truncations, the catalytic domain contains all seven antiparallel strands rather than seven, and like most members of the superfamily, ATP binding is coordinated by the arginine tweezers, located in motifs 2 and 3 (Kaiser et al. 2018).

    Editing in AlaRS occurs at both the pre-transfer and post-transfer levels. Pre-transfer editing occurs through a C-terminal editing domain, which targets mischarged glycyl and seryl groups from tRNAAla (Tsui et al. 1981, Guo et al. 2009b). At the post-transfer level, AlaX proteins (which are homologous with he C-terminal editing domain of AlaRS) also target Ser-tRNAAla (Beebe et al. 2008). This double checkpoint system minimises serine-for-alanline mistranslations, which are known to cause profound problems in nature (Guo et al. 2009a, Schimmel 2011). The C-terminal editing domain is homologous with the N-terminal editing domain of ThrRS (Sankaranarayanan et al. 1999).
