• O-phosphoseryl tRNA synthetase (SepRS) is an enzyme which attaches the non-proteogenic amino acid o-phosphoserine to tRNA-cysteine:

    • Sep + tRNACys + ATP → Sep-tRNACys + AMP + PPi
    Some methanogenic archaea lack the standard CysRS enzyme. In this case, the non-canonical SepRS charges tRNACys with o-phosphoserine, which is modified by Cys-tRNA synthetase to produce cysteine (Sauerwald et al. 2005). Unlike most members of the class II superfamiy, SepRS exists as a homotetramer (Fukunaga and Shigeyuki 2007).

    The catalytic domain of SepRS is similar to that of PylRS and the five PheRS familes. Collectivey, these members constitute subclass IIc (Kavran et al. 2007, Perona et al. 2012, Valencia-Sánchez et al. 2016, Gomez et al. 2020). The catalytic domain itself is quite typical among class II, and contains six antiparallel strands. Like most members of the superfamily, ATP binding is coordinated by the arginine tweezers, located in motifs 2 and 3 (Kaiser et al. 2018). The C-terminal domain binds to the anticodon of tRNACys (Fukunaga and Shigeyuki 2007). It appears that SepRS lacks editing activity (Gomez et al. 2020).
