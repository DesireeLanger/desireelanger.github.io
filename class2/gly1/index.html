• The dimeric GlyRS is part of subclass IIa alongside ProRS, SerRS, ThrRS, and HisRS (Gomez et al. 2020, Valencia-Sánchez et al. 2016, Perona et al. 2012). With the exception of SerRS, the anticodon binding domains of subclass IIa are homologous and reside at the C-terminal (Wolf et al. 1999).

    The domain architecture of GlyRS is minimal, similar to HisRS. There is no editing domain and it appears that GlyRS may lack editing activity altogether (Gomez et al. 2020). Many species possess an insertion domain between motifs 2 and 3 of the catalytic domain. This domain exists in many archaea and eukaryotes, including Saccharomyces cerevisiae, but is lacking in humans. It's functional and structural roles remain unclear.

    GlyRS is present in most archaea, eukaryotes, and some bacteria and is phylogenetically distinct from the tetrameric GlyRS, which is present in most bacteria (Shiba 2005). Some species of yeast possess a second GlyRS gene, which does not contain the lysine rich inserts between motifs 1 and 2 (Chen et al. 2012).
