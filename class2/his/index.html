• Histidyl tRNA synthetase (HisRS) is an enzyme which attaches histidine to its cognate tRNA:
    • His + tRNAHis + ATP → His-tRNAHis + AMP + PPi
    HisRS is part of subclass IIa alongside ProRS, SerRS, ThrRS, and the dimeric GlyRS (Gomez et al. 2020, Valencia-Sánchez et al. 2016, Perona et al. 2012). With the exception of SerRS, the anticodon binding domains of subclass IIa are homologous and reside at the C-terminal (Wolf et al. 1999).

    The C-terminal catalytic domain of the archaeal HisRS is quite typical of a class II aaRS. Like most members of the superfamily, ATP binding is coordinated by the arginine tweezers, located in motifs 2 and 3 (Kaiser et al. 2018). The catalytic domain contains six or seven antiparallel strands. Short urzyme constructs of around 130 amino acids - centered around motifs 1 and 2 - can yield fast catalytic activity, suggesting that the regions downstream of motif 2 may not have been essential for early ancestral class II catalysis (Li et al. 2011).

    It appears that HisRS lacks editing activity (Gomez et al. 2020).
