• The catalytic domain of the Class II amino acyl tRNA synthetase (AARS-II) activates amino acids in order to charge their cognate tRNAs. In total, twelve of the twenty two proteinogenic amino acids are charged by AARS-II. These amino acids are alanine, asparagine, aspartate, cysteine, glycine, histidine, lysine, phenylalaline, proline, pyrrolysine, serine, and threonine. Lysine and cysteine are also processed by the Class I amino acyl tRNA synthetases.     Most AARS-II families are homodimeric. However, PheRS and the bacterial GlyRS exist as heterotetramers, while SepRS is homotetrameric, and the mitochondrial PheRS is monomeric.

    Although they are part of the class II catalytic domain superfamily, the β subunits of the bacterial PheRS and archaeal PheRS families do not in fact catalyse amino acid activation (Klipcan et al. 2020). Instead, catalysis in these enzymes is performed by the respective α subunits PheRS-Bα and PheRS-Aα . As such, these two β chains are lacking the arginine tweezers which would normally coordinate ATP binding; one in motif 2 and the other in motif 3 (Kaiser et al. 2018).

    The common structure of AARS-II is described by three α-helices H1-H3, five β-strands S1-S5, and a strand followed immediately by a helix SH1. The catalytic domain itself is a six stranded anti-parallel β fold. The first five strands are denoted by S1-S5, while the sixth strand is denoted by SH1, as it is immediately followed by a helix. There is also a very short β-strand, typically 2-3 residues in length, which resides within motif 1 and runs parallel with S1. The two arginine tweezers reside in S1 and SH1, which are close proximity with one other. The loops which connect these secondary structural elements are quite variable in length and contain a multitude of family- or clade-specific insertion modules (IM).

    Most AARS-II members contain the same core six antiparallel β-strands (plus a short strand parallel to S1). This anti-parallel fold is bounded between S1 (near the N-terminus) and S3 (>50 residues downstream). However, some members contain additional strands which run parallel with S3. First, the GlyRS-AE insertion module 2 is a small ~40 residue module which contains a β-hairpin. This hairpin runs parallel with S3, and adds an two additional strands to the fold for both dimeric GlyRS families GlyRS-A and GlyRS-E. Second, the subclass 2b insertion module is a ~80 residue module with a short β strand at its N-terminus which runs parallel to S3. This insertion module is found in LysRS-II, AsnRS, AspRS, and AsxRS.
