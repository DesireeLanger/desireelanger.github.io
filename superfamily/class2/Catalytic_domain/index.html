• The catalytic domain of the Class II aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase (AARS-II) activates amino acids so that their cognate tRNA can be charged. In total, thirteen of the 22 proteinogenic amino acids are incorporated into the protein, through 21 distinct AARS-II families; a single representative from each of which is displayed below. These amino acids are alanine, asparagine, aspartate, cysteine, glycine, histidine, lysine, phenylalaline, proline, pyrrolysine, selenocysteine, serine, and threonine. Lysine and cysteine are also incorporated during translation via the Class I amino acyl tRNA synthetases LysRS-I and CysRS.

    Most AARS-II families are homodimeric. However, the cytosolic PheRS and the bacterial GlyRS exist as heterotetramers, while SepRS is homotetrameric, and the mitochondrial PheRS is monomeric.

    Although they are part of the class II catalytic domain superfamily, the β subunits of the bacterial PheRS and archaeal PheRS families do not in fact catalyse amino acid activation (Klipcan et al. 2020). Instead, catalysis in these enzymes is performed by the respective α subunits PheRS-Bα and PheRS-Aα . As such, these two β chains are lacking the arginine tweezers which would normally coordinate ATP binding; one in motif 2 and the other in motif 3 (Kaiser et al. 2018).

    The universal AARS-II structure is composed of 3 α-helices (H1-H3), 5 β-strands (S1-S5) and a strand-helix (SH1). Its catalytic domain is a six-stranded anti-parallel beta fold with the first five strands being denoted as S1-S5 and the sixth strand being part of SH1. There's also a short beta strand within motif 1 that runs parallel with S1. The arginine tweezers are located in S1 and SH1, which are close to each other. The loops connecting these secondary structural elements are variable in length and contain multiple insertion modules (IM).

    All AARS-II members have the same six anti-parallel beta strands bound by S1 (near the N-terminus) and S3 (>50 residues downstream), plus the short strand parallel to S1. However, some members have additional strands parallel to S3. First, GlyRS-AE insertion module 2 is a small (~40 residue) module located between S2 and H3 that contains a β-hairpin running parallel to S3 and adds two additional strands to the fold for both dimeric GlyRS families (GlyRS-A and GlyRS-E). Second, subclass 2b insertion module 1, located between H3 and S3, is found in LysRS-II, AsnRS, AspRS, and AsxRS. It is over 70 residues and contains a short β-strand at its N-terminus which runs parallel to S3. Finally, the AlaRS, HisRS, and ThrRS insertion modules are all located between S3 and S4. These three modules are structurally distinct and appear to have arisen independently. They all contain a short β-strand which runs parallel to S3.

    A β hairpin is located between motifs 1 and 2 in most members of the AARS-II superfamily, with the exception of subclass 2d (AlaRS and GlyRS-Bα). The second part of the hairpin is located at the N-terminus of the protozyme and is universal, while the first strand is responsible for stabilizing the hairpin, and it's likely to have evolved early in the evolutionary history of AARS-II. The hairpin's loop has since seen the appearance of insertion modules for SepRS, SerRS-A, and PheRS-Aα.
