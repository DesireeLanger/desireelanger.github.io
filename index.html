AARS Online

  • The aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases (AARS) are a large group of enzymes that attach amino acids to their cognate tRNA
  • They execute the computational rules of the genetic code
  • Two evolutionary superfamilies: Class I and Class II
  • AARS Online is an interactive tool that documents their sequence, structure, and function
  • Enzymes are sampled from Bacteria , Archaea , Eukaryotes , Organelles , and Viruses
  • Some structures have strong experimental support while others are AlphaFold models
  • Use the evolutionary tree below to navigate to an AARS
